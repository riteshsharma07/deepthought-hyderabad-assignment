# DeepThought Assignment Methodology - Hyderabad Version

## City and Segments Chosen

### City
Hyderabad, Telangana

### Segments
1. Basket A - Specialty Biotech
2. Basket B - Complex APIs / Regulated Pharma

## Why Hyderabad

Hyderabad is one of the strongest Indian hubs for this assignment because it combines:

- Genome Valley and broader life-sciences infrastructure
- a dense ecosystem of biotech, API, and formulation companies
- multiple mid-sized technical manufacturers
- visible growth signals through plant expansion, regulatory activity, and export-oriented pharma operations

This makes Hyderabad a better fit for DeepThought's Federer profile than a city with mostly distributors or low-information manufacturers.

## Research Objective

The goal was to identify companies that are:

- real producers with in-house operations
- based in India and reachable operationally
- technically differentiated
- active in growth sectors
- showing evidence of systems maturity and structured operations

## Research Process

### 1. Longlist Creation

I first built a broad longlist of Hyderabad companies from:

- company websites
- LinkedIn company pages
- industry directories
- pharma and biotech databases
- public news and expansion stories
- cluster references like Genome Valley

The initial list included biotech, diagnostics, API, formulation, and adjacent specialized manufacturers.

### 2. Eligibility Gate Check

Before scoring, I applied the two mandatory gates:

#### E1 - Producer
The company must manufacture a product or run a specialized in-house production capability.

Examples of pass evidence:

- own plant
- manufacturing facility
- cGMP / WHO-GMP / USFDA plant
- in-house biologics or API production
- specialized device or diagnostics manufacturing

Examples of fail evidence:

- pure CRO with no production
- distributor / trader / marketing-only company
- service-only operation with no owned capability

#### E2 - Accessible
The company must have real India operations, ideally Hyderabad HQ, R&D, or production.

### 3. Evidence-Based Scoring

Only companies that passed E1 and E2 were scored on:

- C3 Differentiated
- C4 Decision-Maker Quality
- C5 Growing Sector
- C6 Growth Signals
- C7 Systems Maturity
- C8 Leadership Succession

Scoring used the assignment rubric:

- Weak = 0
- Moderate = half weight
- Strong = full weight

## How the Criteria Were Interpreted

### C3 - Differentiated

Strong evidence included:

- biologics
- monoclonal antibodies
- complex APIs
- specialty injectables
- patented or proprietary processes
- regulated facilities
- technically niche product categories

### C4 - Decision-Maker Quality

Strong evidence included:

- founder with scientific or engineering pedigree
- pharma / biotech domain depth
- clear evidence of building a technical business

### C5 - Growing Sector

This was judged from:

- biotech tailwinds
- regulated-pharma demand
- import substitution
- export opportunity
- India manufacturing momentum

### C6 - Growth Signals

Signals included:

- new plants
- funding or investment
- recent certifications or regulatory milestones
- current hiring or expansion
- fresh website / media activity

### C7 - Systems Maturity

This was inferred using:

- cGMP / WHO-GMP / USFDA references
- ISO or regulated manufacturing environments
- multiple plants
- structured R&D and production footprints

### C8 - Leadership Succession

This was the least visible criterion in quick public research, so I used it conservatively unless there was explicit evidence of:

- next-generation leadership
- layered management
- a strong professional leadership bench

## Sources Used

Main source types:

- company websites
- LinkedIn
- public news reports
- industry directories
- public company / registry references where needed

## Conservative Rules Used

To avoid overclaiming:

1. Companies with unclear manufacturing were downgraded or excluded.
2. Companies with weak public leadership evidence were not over-scored on C4.
3. Companies with limited systems evidence were capped at Moderate for C7.
4. Firms that looked too large, too institutional, or too service-heavy were treated cautiously.

## Why This Hyderabad Approach Is Stronger

Compared with a generic city-wide manufacturing search, Hyderabad gives a better concentration of:

- specialty biotech firms
- regulated-product companies
- science-led businesses
- companies with stronger technical and compliance moats

That makes it easier to find firms that actually resemble DeepThought's ideal customer profile.
